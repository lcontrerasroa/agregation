Epenthesis

As opposed to elision, epenthesis is a phonetic process whereby a sound is inserted. In English, there is often an epenthetic post-nasal plosive (=an extra plosive after a nasal sound), especially in transitions from nasal sounds to plosives or fricatives. The inserted plosive is homorganic with the nasal sound (=they share the same place of articulation).

The most common cases are from /n/ to /s/ (alveolar to alveolar [ n t s ]), from /m/ to /t/ (bilabial to alveolar [ m p t ]), from /m/ to /s/ (bilabial to alveolar [ m p s ]). This happens because a small explosion tends to happen naturally during the transition between these sounds:

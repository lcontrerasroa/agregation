Main sub-rules and exceptions

  1. <a> or <o> in final <alC> or <olC>

/ɑ:/ in GA

<a> 🡪 /ɔː/ if <l> + <l#, t, d, k>

mall(/'mɔːl/), talk(/'tɔːk/), waltz(/'wɔːls/), bald(/'bɔːld/), falcon(/'fɔːlkən/), Baltic(/'bɔːltɪk/)

<a> 🡪 /ɑ:/ if <l> + <m / f>

half(/'hɑːf/), calm(/'kɑːm/), calf(/'kɑːf/), balm(/bɑːm/)

<o> 🡪 /əʊ/ if <l> + <l#, t, d, s, k>

roll(/'rəʊl/), poll(/'pəʊl/), bolt(/'bəʊlt/), told(/'təʊld/), gold(/'gəʊld/), folk(/'fəʊk/), yolk(/'jəʊk/), most(/'məʊst/), host(/'həʊst/), post(/'pəʊst/)

NB: for both <a> and <o> in cases of final <-lk#>, the <l> is silent: walk, talk, folk, yolk…

  1. “w rule” /w/ + <a> and /w/ + <ar>

<w> + <a> 🡪 [wɒ]

/ɑ:/ in GA

water(/'wɒtə/), want(/'wɒnt/), quality(/'kwɒlɪti/), swan(/'swɒn/), swallow(/'swɒləʊ/)

EXCEPT if the following consonant is velar:
quack(/æk/), wax(/æks/), wank(/æŋk/), wag(/æg/)
🡪 regular checked VCC or VC# value.

<w> + <ar> 🡪 [wɔː]

wart(/'wɔːt/), warm(/'wɔːm/), swarm(/'swɔːm/), quarter(/'kwɔːtə/), thwart(/'θwɔːt/)

  1. Special endings:

<-ange#> <-aste#> <-igh(t)#> <-ign#> <-ind#>

V 🡪 free value instead of checked

<a> 🡪 /eɪ/ arrange, change, derange, range, strange… AND ALSO: angel, danger

baste, chaste, haste, paste, taste, waste…

<i> 🡪 /aɪ/ high, nigh, thigh, sigh, bright, tight, fight, flight, knight, right…

align, sign, resign, assign, benign, malign…

bind, blind, kind, find, grind, mind… AND ALSO: child, climb, mild, pint, wild

  1. “pull” rule <f / p / b> + <u> + <-ll / -sh / -tch>

<u> 🡪 /ʊ/

pull, bull, bush, push, butch, putsch…

  1. ASK words <a> + /f, s, θ/ (+ occlusive sourde)

<a> 🡪 /ɑː/

staff, bath, class, glass, grass, pass…
after, craft, draft, graft, raft, shaft, ask, basket, mask, task, gasp, clasp, grasp…

/æ/ in GA

similar endings <-ance#> <-anch#> <-and/t#> <-ample#>

<a> 🡪 /ɑː/

advance, France, chance, dance, glance, branch, ranch, command, demand, grant, plant, example, sample…

  1. “Luick” rule

V + C + V in cases of /100/ (or earlier) stress 🡪 checked value of V

Explanation: When a monographic vowel is in stressed position in the antepenultimate syllable or earlier in the word, it adopts its checked value if followed by a single consonant.

char(/æ/)acter, fed(/e/)eral, personˈal(/æ/)ity, riˈd(/d/)iculous(/ɪ/)

This works for both derived and non-derived words. Thus, a derived word might have a different stressed vowel from its stem:

ˈsan(/ eɪ /)e 🡪 ˈsan(/ æ /)ity, ˈspher(/ ɪə /)e 🡪 ˈspher(/ e /)ical, ˈbib(/ aɪ /)le 🡪 ˈbib(/ ɪ /)lical

HOWEVER: Many exceptions to this rule. Sometimes, isomorphism (=keeping the same form) is stronger:

ˈfav(/ eɪ /)or 🡪 ˈfav(/ eɪ /)orite, ˈrar(/ eə /)e 🡪 ˈrar(/ eə /)ity, ˈglor(/ ɔː /)y 🡪 ˈglor(/ ɔː /)ify, ˈmot(/ əʊ /)ive 🡪 ˈmot(/ əʊ /)ivate

  1. Special pronunciations of <o>

<o> 🡪 / ʌ/ son, front, company, monk, month, sponge, tongue, wonder, worry, come, glove, love, other, brother...

<o> 🡪 /u:/ do, to, two, who, lose, move, prove, tomb, womb…

Unstressed vowels

  1. General rules

<a, e, o, u> 🡪 / ə / 'salad, 'amulet, 'pilot, 'corpus

<a, e, i> 🡪 / ɪ / 'cottage, 'budget, 'stupid

<u> 🡪 / (j)ʊ / 'educate, 'circular, 'fabulous

  1. In pre-tonic contexts

V + 'V 🡪 free value of vowel re(/iː/)'ality, zo(/uː/)'ology, i'(/aɪ/)ambic

V + 'C 🡪 regular reduced value a(/ə/)'mend, se(/ə/)'rene, a(/ə/)'tomic

V + CC 🡪 checked value um(/ʌ/)'brella, dog(/ɒ/)'matic, par(/ɑː/)'tition 🡪 (r-modification)

  1. In final V# / VC0 contexts (cases of final <i, y> or cases of diaeresis)

<a> 🡪 / ə / 'sofa(/'səʊfə/), hy'ena(/haɪ'iːnə/)

<e, i / y > 🡪 / i / 'acne(/'ækni/), oce'anic(/ˌəʊʃiˈænɪk/), 'happy(/ˈhæpi/)

<u> 🡪 / u / 'influence(/ˈɪnfluəns/), situation(/ˌsɪtʃuˈeɪʃən/)

Latin and Greek <oe> (and also <ae>)

In SBE, words of Latin and Greek origin with <oe> and also <ae> are usually pronounced as / iː/ amoeba, foetus, Caesar, haematoma, encyclopaedia.
Exception: / e / haemorrhage, haemorrhoid

In GA, most of these digraphs have been simplified to monographs:

/ iː/ ameba, fetus, Cesar, hematoma, encyclopedia.
🡪 subpoena, oenophile are still used, though.

There are three common minimal pairs which vary in pronunciation depending on their meaning:

<bow> /baʊ/ (v.) s'incliner vs. /bəʊ/ (n.) arc, ruban

<sow> /saʊ/ (n.) truie vs. /səʊ/ (v.) semer

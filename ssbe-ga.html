5. SSBE and GA

Rhoticity

SSBE is a non-rhotic accent whereas GA is rhotic. This means that in SSBE the phoneme /r/ is only produced in onset position, whereas in GA it can appear in both onset and coda position. This has the following consequences on vowels:

R-modified vowels

SSBE GA
/ɑː/ /ɑːr/ or /ɑr/
/ɜː/ /ɜːr/ or /ɝː/ or /ɜr/ or /ɝ/
/ɔː/ /ɔːr/ or /ɔr/
/ə/ /ər/ or /ɚ/

Heart 🡪 /ˈhɑːt/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈhɑrt, ˈhɑːrt/ (GA)
Bird 🡪 /ˈbɜːd/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈbɜːrd, ˈbɝːd, ˈbɜrd, ˈbɝd/ (GA)
Order 🡪 /ˈɔːdə/ (SSBE) vs. / ˈɔːrdər, ˈɔrdər, ˈɔːrdɚ, ˈɔrdɚ/ (GA)
Inner 🡪 /ˈɪnə/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈɪnər, ˈɪnɚ/ (GA)

SSBE GA

/eə/ /e(ə)r/

T-voicing

In post-stressed position ('σ σ), onset /t/ tends to get voiced in GA (it sounds closer to /d/). This is represented by adding the “voiced” diacritic of the IPA : / t̬ /:

Better 🡪 /ˈbetə/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈbet̬ɚ/ (GA) After a vowel
Garter 🡪 /ˈɡɑːtə/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈɡɑːrt̬ɚ/ (GA) After an r-modified vowel
International 🡪 /ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl/ vs. /ˌɪnt̬ɚˈnæʃənəl/ After /n/

This phenomenon can go past words boundaries # with the resyllabification of /t/ (linked /t/, cf. Linking / sandhi / liaison above):

Hit it 🡪 /ˈhɪt ɪt/ vs. /ˈhɪt̬ ɪt/
She sat on the chair 🡪 /ʃi ˈsæt ɒn ðə ˈtʃeə/ vs. /ʃi ˈsæt̬ ɑn ðə ˈtʃer/

COT-CAUGHT merger

The /ɒ - ɔː / distinction of SSBE is neutralized to /ɑː or ɑ/ (any) in GA:

Cot 🡪 /ˈkɒt/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈkɑːt/ (GA)
Caught 🡪 /ˈkɔːt/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈkɑːt/ (GA)
Dog 🡪 /ˈdɒg/ vs. /ˈdɑːg/
Walk 🡪 /ˈwɔːk/ vs. /ˈwɑːk/

Be careful!: This does not affect /ɔː/ in Vr or Vre contexts, i.e. /ɔːr/ is still present in GA.

North 🡪 /ˈnɔːθ/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈnɔːrθ/ (GA)
More 🡪 /ˈmɔː/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈmɔːr/ (GA)

GOAT vowel

The diphthong in GOAT is produced as /əʊ/ in SSBE and as /oʊ/ in GA.

Hello 🡪 /həˈləʊ/ (SSBE) vs. /həˈloʊ/ (GA)

ASK vowel

In SSBE, <a> is pronounced as the open back unrounded vowel /ɑː/ before /f, s, θ/ as well as in endings <-ance#> <-anch#> <-and/t#> <-ample#> (cf. ASK words <a> + /f, s, θ/ (+ occlusive sourde) above).

In GA, this special subrule is lost and <a> is pronounced as /æ/ in these cases, as in any other VCC or VC# context.

Last 🡪 /ˈlɑːst/ (SSBE) vs. /ˈlæst/ (GA)
France 🡪 /ˈfrɑːns/ vs. /ˈfræns/
command 🡪 /kəˈmɑːnd/ vs. /kəˈmænd/

Yod-dropping

In SSBE, stressed /uː/ is usually preceded by a yod /j/ in 'Cuː contexts 🡪 'Cjuː

tune /ˈtjuːn/, cute /ˈkjuːt/, humid /ˈhjuːmɪd/, feud /ˈfjuːd/, pew /ˈpjuː/, duty /ˈdjuːti/

This also tends to happen in GA, but there is an ongoing process of yod-dropping, particularly after alveolars /t, d, s, z, n/. Therefore:

tune /ˈtuːn/, duty /ˈduːt̬i/, suicide /ˈsuːəsaɪd/, presume /prəˈzuːm/, new /ˈnuː/

Schwa over /ɪ/

In unstressed position, <e, a, i>, usually pronounced as /ɪ/ in SSBE (cf. Unstressed vowels above), tend to be centralized more in GA, and pronounced as /ə/.

versatile 🡪 /ˈvɝːsət̬əl
continental 🡪 /ˌkɑːnt̬əˈnent̬əl/
digital 🡪 /ˈdɪdʒət̬əl/
